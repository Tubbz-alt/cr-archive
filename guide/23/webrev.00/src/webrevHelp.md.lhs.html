 3 So, what is webrev?
 4 
 5 `webrev` refers to both the tool and its output. The script,
 6 [`webrev.ksh`](http://hg.openjdk.java.net/code-tools/webrev/raw-file/tip/webrev.ksh),
 7 is maintained in the [Code Tools](../projects/code-tools) Project.
 8 The latest version of the tool may be downloaded from
 9 [http://hg.openjdk.java.net/code-tools/webrev/raw-file/tip/webrev.ksh](http://hg.openjdk.java.net/code-tools/webrev/raw-file/tip/webrev.ksh).
10 
11 `webrev.ksh` crawls over your changes to generate a
12 set of web-based views of the differences in your code. The different views
13 allow reviewers to easily look at your proposed changes, selecting the
14 appropriate difference format based on the type of file, the type of change,
15 and their own preferences.
16 
17 ## Examples
18 
19 `ksh ./webrev.ksh -N -r <REV>`
20 
21 >    Generate diffs between revision _`<REV>`_ and the `tip`. Output created under the
22 >    `./webrev/` directory.
23 
24 `ksh ./webrev.ksh -m -N -c <BUGID>`
25 
26 >    Force the use of Mercurial to generate diffs between all
27 >    modified files and the current `tip`. At the top of
28 >    the main page Include a clickable link to _`<BUGID>`_ in the
29 >    [OpenJDK Bug System](http://bugs.openjdk.java.net). Output to `./webrev/`.
30 
31 `ksh WDIR=<OUTDIR> ./webrev.ksh <FILE>`
32 
33 >    Useful when generating diffs across multiple repositories in
34 >    the forest, the _`<FILE>`_
35 >    contains a list of files to diff. Output to alternate location
36 >    _`<OUTDIR>`_.
37 
38 `ksh ./webrev.ksh -help`
39 
40 >    Complete list of options.
