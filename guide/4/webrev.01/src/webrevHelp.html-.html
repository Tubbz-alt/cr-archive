13 <h1>Webrev Help</h1>
14 
15 <p> So, what is webrev? </p>
16 
17 <p> <tt>webrev</tt> refers to both the tool and its output.  The
18 script, <a href="http://hg.openjdk.java.net/code-tools/webrev/raw-file/tip/webrev.ksh"><tt>webrev.ksh</tt></a>,
19 is maintained in the <a href="../projects/code-tools">Code Tools</a>
20 Project. The latest version of the tool may be downloaded from 
21 <a href="http://hg.openjdk.java.net/code-tools/webrev/raw-file/tip/webrev.ksh">http://hg.openjdk.java.net/code-tools/webrev/raw-file/tip/webrev.ksh</a>. </p>
22 
23 <p> <tt>webrev.ksh</tt> crawls over your changes to generate a set of
24 web-based views of the differences in your code.  The different views allow
25 reviewers to easily look at your proposed changes, selecting the appropriate
26 difference format based on the type of file, the type of change, and their own
27 preferences. </p>
28 
29 <h2>Examples</h2>
30 
31 <dl>
32 <dt> <tt>ksh ./webrev.ksh -N -r &lt;REV&gt; </tt> </dt>
33 <dd> <br /> Generate diffs between revision <em><tt>&lt;REV&gt;</tt></em> and
34   the <tt>tip</tt>. Output created under the <tt>./webrev/</tt>
35   directory. </dd>
36 </dl>
37 
38 <dl>
39 <dt> <tt>ksh ./webrev.ksh -m -N -c &lt;BUGID&gt;</tt></dt>
40 <dd> <br /> Force the use of Mercurial to generate diffs between all modified
41   files and the current <tt>tip</tt>.  At the top of the main page Include a
42   clickable link to <em><tt>&lt;BUGID&gt;</tt></em> in the 
43   <a href="http://bugs.openjdk.java.net">OpenJDK Bug System</a>.
44   Output to <tt>./webrev/</tt>. </dd>
45 </dl>
46 
47 <dl>
48 <dt> <tt>ksh WDIR=&lt;OUTDIR&gt; ./webrev.ksh &lt;FILE&gt;</tt></dt>
49 <dd> <br /> Useful when generating diffs across multiple repositories in the
50   forest, the <em><tt>&lt;FILE&gt;</tt></em> contains a list of files to
51   diff.  Output to alternate location <em><tt>&lt;OUTDIR&gt;</tt></em>. </dd>
52 </dl>
53 
54 <dl>
55 <dt> <tt>ksh ./webrev.ksh -help</tt></dt>
56 <dd> <br /> Complete list of options. </dd>
57 </dl>
