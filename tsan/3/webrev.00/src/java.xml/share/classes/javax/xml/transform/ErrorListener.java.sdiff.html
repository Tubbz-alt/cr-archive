 33  * interface.</p>
 34  *
 35  * <p>If an application does <em>not</em> register its own custom
 36  * <code>ErrorListener</code>, the default <code>ErrorListener</code>

 38  * and does not throw any <code>Exception</code>s.

 48  * after a call to {@link #fatalError(TransformerException exception)}.</p>

 28 /**
 29  * The listener interface used by a {@link TransformerFactory} or {@link Transformer}
 30  * to notify callers of error messages that occur during a transformation process.
 31  * An ErrorListener receives three levels of messages: warnings, errors and fatal
 32  * errors as classified by their severity. Each of them is handled as described
 33  * in their respective method.
 34  *
 35  * <p>
 36  * An ErrorListener instance can be registered to a {@link TransformerFactory}
 37  * or {@link Transformer} through
 38  * the {@link TransformerFactory#setErrorListener(ErrorListener)}
 39  * or {@link Transformer#setErrorListener(ErrorListener)}
 40  * method to receive errors and warnings reported by the TransformerFactory
 41  * or Transformer.
 42  *
 43  * <p>
 44  * When a listener is registered, the {@link TransformerFactory} or {@link Transformer}
 45  * must use this interface to pass on all warnings and errors to the listener
 46  * and let the application decide how to handle them.
 47  * Note that the {@code TransformerFactory} or {@code Transformer} is not
 48  * required to continue with the transformation after a call to
 49  * {@link #fatalError(TransformerException exception)}.
 50  *
 51  * <p>
 52  * If an application does not provide a listener, the {@link TransformerFactory}
 53  * or {@link Transformer} shall create one on its own. The default {@code ErrorListener}
 54  * may take no action for warnings and recoverable errors, and allow the
 55  * transformation to continue.
 56  * However, the {@code TransformerFactory} or {@code Transformer} may still throw
 57  * {@code TransformerException} when it decides it can not continue processing.
 58  *
 59  * @apiNote It is recommended that applications register and use their own
 60  * {@code ErrorListener} to override the default behavior in order to ensure
 61  * proper handling of warnings and errors.
 62  *
 63  * @since 1.4
 64  */
